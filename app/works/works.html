Alchemy Integrative Wellness

Responsive, mobile first,
WordPress, newsletter,
PHP, HTML5, CSS3
Accessibility

Chrysta Faye wanted a modern website that she could manage. A top priority, of course, was that her new site be responsive and streamlined for mobile use.

Since Chrysta has experience managing a WordPress website, we chose the ubiquitous content management system(CMS) upon which to build the site. Another priority was a call to action for newsletter subscriptions. We decided on a two - column design, with a large header to feature artwork to complement her alchemical aesthetic.

Initial wireframing was done with ultra - high - tech pencil and graph paper.

In my process, an eraser (and Ctrl + Z) is essential. I also like how it presents the wireframe as a sketch that can be easily edited.

Once the design was approved, I took the twenty - sixteen starter theme and massaged it into shape. In my opinion, we might as well have the folks at WordPress.org performing security updates for us, so Chrysta's site is a child-theme of the current default WordPress theme. It might be a bit more work than using pre-built themes more similar to our goal, but, since WordPress is the most commonly-used CMS on the web it gets targeted by all sorts of nefarious actors.

The mandala background image and logo was created by Kat V.L.Wright, who is an incredible designer and illustrator, and also happens to be my wife.

Caitlin Sabo Photography


WordPress, Multilingual,
PHP, HTML, CSS

Caitlin Sabo is an American fine art photographer based in Mexico City. She wanted an elegant website to present her internationally recognized photography.

Caitlin, being a visual artist, had a very specific idea of what she wanted. She wanted a black background, a minimalistic gallery, and a spacious layout. Because her intentions were clearly conveyed, visually, we skipped the traditional wireframing step, moving on to some mockups I made in PhotoShop, which mostly played with typographic elements.

Once the design was approved and built to match the mockup (starting from the skeletal Bartheleme theme), I started working on some modifications to the backend so that Caitlin would have an easy time managing her webiste. I modified the theme's structure so that the traditional WordPress post system would instead add a new image gallery to her portfolios page.

Finally, since Caitlin is multilingual herself and exhibits in Spain, the USA, Mexico and Italy, we created both Spanish and Italian versions of the site. This required a smarter gallery plugin. To get the right feel, I added additional functionality to the gallery for the new languages

The Missing Parts


Vanilla JS, Flash,
HTML, CSS,
Graphic Design,
Sculpture, Guitar
Blood, Sweat and Tears

Among many other passions, I also play music with The Missing Parts. The best description to date for the music is "Folk Music from an Undiscovered Country, and so I took a plaster casting of a friend's face and painted it to look like a map. Photos of the sculpture are featured heavily in the site.

I chose a single-page site using JavaScript and a lot of absolute positioning. A one-page site made sense because I wanted listeners to be able to browse while playing music in the site. I built a custom Flash player with a skin featuring a second photo of the map/mask sculpture.

I built the site from a photoshop mockup. Every pixel had to line up for it to work.

A bit of JavaScript handles tour dates though at the moment we aren't playing shows.
